Recyclable Items Guide

Plastic

Plastic is one of the most commonly used materials, but it can take hundreds of years to decompose. Recycling plastic reduces the need for new plastic production, saving energy and reducing pollution.

Examples:
• Plastic Bottles
• Plastic Bags
• Food Containers
• Plastic Cups

Recycling Tips:
• Rinse containers before recycling.
• Avoid mixing plastic bags with other recyclables.
• Flatten plastic bottles to save space.

Paper

Recycling paper saves trees and energy. Paper can be recycled multiple times to produce new products like newspapers, books, and packaging.

Examples:
• Newspapers
• Cardboard
• Magazines
• Paper Packaging

Recycling Tips:
• Remove plastic and metal from paper products.
• Flatten cardboard boxes to save space.
• Avoid recycling tissues or paper towels.

Glass

Glass is 100% recyclable and can be recycled endlessly without losing quality. It saves raw materials and energy, making it one of the most eco-friendly materials.

Examples:
• Glass Bottles
• Glass Jars
• Glass Containers

Recycling Tips:
• Rinse glass containers before recycling.
• Keep glass free from food residues.
• Separate colored glass from clear glass when possible.

Metal

Metals like aluminum and steel can be recycled repeatedly without losing quality. Recycling metals saves natural resources and reduces the energy needed to extract and process raw materials.

Examples:
• Aluminum Cans
• Steel Cans
• Tin Foil

Recycling Tips:
• Rinse cans before recycling.
• Avoid mixing different types of metals.
• Flatten aluminum cans to save space.
